## Tweet 2/10 - The Founder's Usual Problem

Most founders operate on this loop:

- **9 AM:** Wake up to fire (Slack, email, customer support)
- **10 AM - 6 PM:** React to the biggest fires
- **6 PM:** Realize you didn't do strategic work
- **7 PM - midnight:** Caffeinated catch-up
- **Midnight:** Pass out
- **Repeat**

You're working 15 hours to move 1 inch forward.

Meanwhile, your competition is sleeping better AND growing faster.

Why? Because they've delegated the reactivity.

## Tweet 4/10 - The Economics of Always-On

Let's think about what 24/7 really means:

**Traditional business:**
- Founder works 12 hours/day → 12 hours of operations
- Cost: Founder's $1M+ annual salary equivalent
- Weekend: Operations stop completely
- Monthly "ops ceiling": ~250 productive hours

**Agent-based business:**
- Founder works 6 strategic hours/day (peak focus)
- Agent works 24 hours/day (repetitive + mechanical tasks)
- Cost: $0 (local compute) to $50/month (cloud)
- Weekend: Operations continue uninterrupted
- Monthly "ops ceiling": 730 potential hours (+ founder's 180)

You just tripled your available capacity for 1% cost.
